## Required outcome

Create a production-ready repository contract that lets multiple agents work safely in
parallel and complete routine branch-local work without repeated Product Owner approvals,
while keeping system access, consequential effects, acceptance, and `main` merge under
human authority.

The contract must be understandable to workers, enforceable through machine-readable
records and validation, resistant to status drift and concurrent control-file edits, and
suitable for independent Claude review.

## Acceptance criteria

1. Every worker instruction is blocked until canonical and operational GitHub state is
   synchronized, including known local-but-unpushed work.
2. Parallel tasks have isolated branches/worktrees, exclusive writable ownership,
   dependencies, shared surfaces, and integration order.
3. Approved task envelopes authorize independent investigation, implementation,
   validation, correction, commit, push, and draft-PR updates without repeated approval.
4. Product Owner authority for scope, risk, functional acceptance, merge, and release is
   explicit and cannot be delegated by a worker.
5. Instructions define precise outcomes and boundaries without prematurely prescribing the
   method.
6. Public task-scoped downloads and isolated dependencies are allowed with complete
   provenance; system/personal/credentialed/privileged/destructive/external actions remain
   approval-gated.
7. Active task packets cannot be concurrently rewritten after execution begins; amendments
   are separate and require resynchronization.
8. Worker output is both human-readable and machine-readable for ChatGPT review.
9. Canonical adapters, roles, workflows, routing, decisions, and current status agree.
10. Automated validation checks structural alignment and representative handoff validity.
11. No runtime, product behavior, Sprint 5 implementation, policy-engine, Hermes activation,
    HELIX, SGN-01, or ARCH-001 content is implemented or accepted.
12. A draft PR and complete evidence are ready for Claude's separate four-eyes review.

## Method-selection record

The System Architect investigated the existing authority order, workflows, role adapters,
Event Horizon decisions, public-repository policy, evidence policy, open work, and the
GOV-004 failure sequence. The selected design separates:

- human-readable policy;
- machine-readable active ownership and handoff contracts;
- thin role adapters;
- immutable execution authority and separate amendments;
- automated structural validation.

This preserves independent worker method selection while making safety boundaries and
integration behavior deterministic.
